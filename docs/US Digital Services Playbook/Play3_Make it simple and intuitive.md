## Play 3
### Make it simple and intuitive



Using a government service shouldn’t be stressful, confusing, or daunting. It’s our job to build services that are simple and intuitive enough that users succeed the first time, unaided.

#### checklist
- [ ] Create or use an existing, simple, and flexible design style guide for the service
* Yes. We used open source design style sheet named [Singlepaged] (https://github.com/t413/SinglePaged)  

- [ ] Use the design style guide consistently for related digital services
* Yes. We used singlepaged theme for our internal application. So choosen this style guide for this prototype.

- [ ] Give users clear information about where they are in each step of the process
* We provided Navigation bar at top for clear indication. [usfoodrecall.com] (http://www.usfoodrecall.com/)

- [ ] Follow accessibility best practices to ensure all people can use the service
* We took help from our experienced Usability Tester to design site that can be understood by all age grouped people.

- [ ] Provide users with a way to exit and return later to complete the process
* We consolidated all functionality in single page. and also can navigate through top menu bar.

- [ ] Use language that is familiar to the user and easy to understand
* We took help from our content designer and language expert to use simple english in site. Mostly we used graphics(images) 
to make easily understandable.

- [ ] Use language and design consistently throughout the service, including online and offline touch points
* We gave high importance to consistency, all our prototype sections followed same style patterns.
